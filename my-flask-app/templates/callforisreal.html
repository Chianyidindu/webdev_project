A Call for Solidarity & Support

Individuals and communities worldwide have numerous avenues to demonstrate their support for Israel, its people, and its continued growth and development. Whether by volunteering your time and skills, providing financial aid, or engaging in constructive advocacy, every form of support can create a meaningful impact.

This page aims to provide comprehensive information on various ways to volunteer, donate, and connect with organizations relevant to supporting Israel. We also include specific details for inquiries and contact points within Abuja, Nigeria.

How You Can Help

Volunteering Opportunities

A multitude of organizations facilitate international volunteers wishing to contribute their expertise and time in Israel. Opportunities span various sectors and commitment levels:

  • Community & Social Work: Assist in community centers, mentor youth, or support social welfare initiatives.
  • Agriculture & Kibbutz Life: Experience life on a kibbutz or help on farms, particularly during harvest seasons. Organizations like Leket Israel often seek volunteers for food rescue and agricultural projects.
  • Healthcare Professionals: Qualified medical and healthcare workers may find opportunities in hospitals, clinics, or support roles.
  • Environmental Conservation: Join projects dedicated to preserving Israel's unique natural landscapes and biodiversity.
  • Educational Support: Contribute to formal or informal educational programs, tutoring, or youth activities.
  • Support for Vulnerable Groups: Work with organizations aiding the elderly, new immigrants (Olim), or individuals with disabilities.

Explore programs on platforms such as Volunteer World and GoAbroad. Additionally, organizations like the UJA-Federation of New York often list or support volunteer missions. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) offers specific volunteer programs for overseas participants, such as Sar-El.

Making Donations

Financial contributions are crucial for supporting a diverse range of causes in Israel, including humanitarian relief, social services, and development projects. Consider these avenues:

  • Humanitarian Aid: Organizations like the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) and Magen David Adom (Israel's national emergency medical, disaster, ambulance, and blood bank service) often have active appeals for the region.
  • Support for Soldiers & Families: Friends of the Israel Defense Forces (FIDF) provides well-being and educational programs for IDF soldiers and their families.
  • Social Welfare & Development: Numerous non-profits focus on critical areas like poverty alleviation, education enhancement, and healthcare access. It's important to research and choose organizations that align with your philanthropic goals.
  • Community & Advocacy: Groups like Christians United for Israel (CUFI) and local Jewish Federations often direct funds to various community projects and advocacy efforts.

When donating, always verify the legitimacy of the organization. Use official websites and secure payment platforms. Ensure transparency regarding how donations are utilized.

Inquiries & Support from Abuja, Nigeria

Embassy of Israel in Abuja

For official inquiries, information regarding bilateral initiatives, or guidance on supporting Israel-related projects from Nigeria, the primary contact is the Embassy of Israel in Abuja.

Address: Plot 12, Mary Slessor Street, Off Udo Udoma Crescent, Asokoro, Abuja, Federal Capital Territory, Nigeria.

General Phone: (+234) 0209-4605500

Consular Service Phone: (+234) 0209-4605515

General Email: info@abuja.mfa.gov.il

Consular Email: consular-service@abuja.mfa.gov.il

Official Website: new.embassies.gov.il/nigeria/en

It is recommended to visit the embassy's official website or call in advance to confirm office hours, schedule appointments (especially for consular matters), and inquire about specific services.

The Embassy can offer information on cultural exchanges, development partnerships, and official channels for providing support. They might also connect you with Nigerian-based community organizations or friendship associations involved in fostering Nigeria-Israel relations and support initiatives.

While locating specific local NGOs in Abuja solely focused on Israel-related volunteering or donations might require more in-depth local research, the Embassy of Israel serves as the most reliable initial point of contact for verified information and guidance.
